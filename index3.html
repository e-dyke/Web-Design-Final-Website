ARTIST STATEMENT

My art serves as a personal escape from the bitterness life can bring. When I create, I feel the most at peace, and I want to give my audience the opportunity to feel that as well. My art focuses on all the beauty that remains in this purposefully crafted world. The mediums I gravitate towards are ceramics and painting. I consider my style of painting a middle ground between realism and impressionism. My designs emerge from radiant color and brushstrokes, both soft and bold, to refine or highlight depending on the area. Painting has always felt like my safe place.

However, in my freshman year of college, I was introduced to ceramics. From the very first class, I was captivated. Since then, I have worked to combine both of these artistic outlets. Creating functional pieces of pottery from a mere lump of clay is fascinating to me. I get to highlight nature in the most full-circle way—portraying nature's beauty with materials from nature itself. To glaze my pottery, I use a white mineral glaze as a base, just like a painter priming a canvas. Then, I paint on the surface with underglaze. My technique, natural subject matter, and choice of color all aid in my artistic message that this world is still light and vibrant. We are surrounded by its brilliance, and if we take the time to be still and observe, we will realize and appreciate it more. My interpretations aim to reflect how wonderfully and meticulously crafted every inch of this world is. I strive to see as much of this world as I am able to in my lifetime, and to share it with others through my art.
