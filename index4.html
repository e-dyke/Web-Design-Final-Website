ARTIST BIO

Elaina will receive her Bachelor of Arts in Art from Columbia College on May 2, 2026. She is a multifaceted artist that works in painting, ceramics, and jewelry. Her work primarily reflects nature. She is a member of the Honors Program at Columbia College, and has previously been a member of the Hannibal Arts Council, and the National Art Honors Society. Her works have been exhibited at the Hannibal Arts Council, Quincy Art Center, Columbia College Sydney Larson Gallery, and numerous other universities across Missouri. She will continue to practice in Missouri via freelance artistry after graduation.

MY STORY

From the moment I could hold a crayon in my hand, I would draw. I have so many memories from each summer of my childhood, where my mom and I would spend hours drawing with sidewalk chalk in our driveway. We loved coloring in coloring books together while playing Disney movies in the background. I have always had such a large imagination, making up stories, going on pretend adventures, and crafting. Though, at this age I was just purely having fun--I had no idea how art would continue to follow me through my life.

Throughout elementary and middle school, I was always the student that would go above and beyond on class projects, group posters--basically anything involving a layout, design, and creativity. I was obsessed with creating, and putting my all into every project I was given. My family is full of artists, and I began taking watercolor lessons from my aunt Nancy and cousin Kelly--it was SO fun! Art was very quickly becoming an all-consuming hobby. In 8th grade, I remember having this realization that art was something I was passionate about. I could see it in myself, and my teachers could too. My art teacher encouraged me to take art classes in high school, telling me she saw my potential. I was so excited to feel praise by someone other than close family, who tends to tell their child that everything they do is amazing... which I appreciate! However, knowing that people without such a bias also thought I had this artistic skill, made me feel incredible.

In my freshman year of highschool, I began taking at least two art classes a semster. I learned about proper techniques, proportions, different mediums of creation, and how to refine my skillset. I primarily created graphite/charcoal drawings, colored pencil, and acrylic paintings. My sophopmore year, I learned about printmaking and created my own lino-cut. In my junior year, I took my first sculpture class. I was absolutely captivated. We learned the basics of handbuilding, first creating a simple pinch pot, and evolving into sculpture--creating mugs, boxes with lids, a teapot, and even an animal head. I learned that I not only enjoyed working in a 2 dimensional manner, but also 3 dimensionally, and I was quite good! My senior year I continued creating sculptures, paintings, drawings, as well as starting to work with adobe. I quickly learned that graphic design was not for me. I much preferred working with something tangible, and using my hands. In my last high school years began the discussions of, "What are you going to do with your life?" To be honest, I had no clue. I knew I loved art more than anything else, but how would I make a career out of it? Was that even possible? I was so doubtful, but I was able to have in-depth discussions with my art teachers and guidance counselors. My teachers encouraged me not to give up on art just because it seemed like a scary career. I learned about all of the different career opportunities there were such as teaching, becoming an art professor, starting my own business, becoming a freelance artist. My worries started to slip away. I loved the idea of having such creative freedom, and creating for both my own happiness, but also the happiness of others. I decided to commit to this idea and see where it would take me.

I applied to 3 schools in Missouri that were ideal in both location and scale of their art program. Ultimately, I chose Columbia College of Missouri, and I could not have made a better choice. I also learned that based on the dual credit I completed in highschool, that I would be able to complete my BA in Art in just 3 years! My freshman year, I took my first ceramics class. I was absolutely captivated. Taking a mere ball of clay, manipulating it on a pottery wheel, and ultimately creating a functional piece of pottery was such a fascinating process to me. I learned to create small pieces such as pots, mugs, and trays, as well as how the trimming, firing, and glazing process worked. The following year, I took my first jewelry class. I learned about processes such as cutting metal with a jeweler's saw, soldering, creating different surface finishes, stone setting, and sizing rings. I grew such a love for this as well! Throughout my last two years of college, I have continued to practice and improve my skills in painting, jewelry, and ceramics. In ceramics, I learned about new glaing techniques, and found a love for painting on my ceramics with underglazes, almost treating them as if they are canvases. Now, I had 3 different mediums that I loved working in, and could see myself doing forever. How would I choose? However, I began to have a realization... why would I HAVE to choose? I realized that I did not want to give up any of these mediums to be a single kind of artist. I am multifaceted, and I always have been. Which is why I decided to work for myself. I want to continue to create what brings me joy after college, and be able to share my talents and that joy with others. I want to make a business for myself, and make art that is true to me for as long as I am able--so thank YOU for being a part of the beginning of my story, and I am so excited for what the future holds for me.
